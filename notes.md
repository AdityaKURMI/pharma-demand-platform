Investigation 1 — suppression by type: hypothesis confirmed. MCOU rows are suppressed at 54.9% vs FFSU at 31.4%. So managed-care data isn't just smaller in row count — more than half of it is privacy-redacted. Combined with the Medi-Cal Rx carve-out story (pharmacy moved back to state-paid FFS in CA), the picture is now coherent: in California 2023, FFS is where the real pharmacy volume lives, and the MCOU remainder is fragmented, low-count, heavily suppressed residue. Write exactly that in notes.md. The methodological takeaway for the platform: suppression is not random — it varies by payment type (and will vary by state and by drug), so any handling of suppressed rows has to account for that, and the paper should show this table.

Investigation 2 — concentration: prediction landed almost exactly. Top 500 of 3,969 drug names = 92.3% of all prescription volume. Even top 50 covers nearly half. Classic power-law. This single number makes a major scoping decision for you, defensibly: forecasting the top ~500 drug concepts captures >92% of demand — so the ML phase works on 500 series per state, not 4,000, and you have a measured justification rather than an arbitrary cutoff. One honest caveat to note alongside it: this was computed on the truncated 10-char product_name, which merges some distinct drugs and splits none — after proper NDC resolution in Phase 2, the real number will shift slightly (probably slightly more concentrated). Recomputing it then is a nice before/after result for the paper.
